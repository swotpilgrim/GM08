## Chapter 16: Twenty Years of Murder
**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words
**POV:** Leon Kowalski, third person limited
**Setting:** WKNC station building—working area with organized evidence
**Time:** Early morning

**Purpose:** Present complete chronological analysis of Raymond's murders, establishing pattern and methodology to understand the systematic evil they're confronting.

**Beats:**
- Leon organizes all evidence chronologically using his detail-obsessed methodology; first murder: 1963, Thomas Hendricks threatened to report spliced broadcasts to FCC
- Raymond killed Hendricks, discovered he could preserve voices through recording before death, began deliberately selecting victims based on isolation and voice quality

- Group calculates Raymond is functionally intelligent, patient, organized—methodical killer hiding behind beloved public persona; question: where is he now and when will he return?

**Dialogue Topics:** Timeline construction, first murder as turning point, evolution of methods, psychological profile of organized serial killer, Raymond's intelligence and planning, debate over whether he's still nearby watching.

**Emotional Arc:** Leon's conspiracy theorist skills validated create pride mixed with horror. Group transitions from reactive fear to analytical understanding—knowing enemy helps them plan survival.

**Key Details:** Hand-drawn timeline, twenty-year span, Margaret's 1961 death as trigger, Thomas Hendricks' 1963 murder, victim selection criteria evolution, burial markers, audio editing techniques improving.
